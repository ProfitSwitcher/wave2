# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners and small business owners across Western Montana (Missoula, Seeley Lake, Great Falls, Helena, Kalispell, Bozeman) who need heating, cooling, plumbing, or electrical work. They're busy, they discover problems mid-day, and they want to book quickly — ideally without calling. Many are first-time homeowners who don't know the jargon. They value reliability, fair pricing, and someone who shows up on time.

## Product Purpose

Alvis WAVE Services is a full-service home services contractor (formerly Alvis Electrical) that now covers Water (plumbing), Air (HVAC), Voltage (electrical), and Experts (remodeling). The website's primary job is to convert visitors into booked appointments — minimizing phone calls by making offers clear, services easy to browse, and the booking path frictionless. Secondary jobs: SEO capture (city-specific pages), trust-building (reviews, financing, guarantees), and marketing program awareness (yard sign program, filter saver club, maintenance plans).

## Brand Personality

trustworthy, local, no-nonsense

Real Montana tradespeople. Not a faceless corporation. Not a slick tech startup. The kind of company that fixes your problem, doesn't upsell you on something you don't need, and leaves your house cleaner than they found it. Speaks plainly. No fluff. Confident but not arrogant.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient hero sections and abstract illustrations
- Anything that feels "AI-generated" — overly symmetrical, sterile, or corporate
- The "big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent" SaaS cliché
- Stock photo families smiling at laptops
- Excessive card grids with icon + heading + text repeated endlessly
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, side-stripe card borders
- Over-styling that prioritizes "cool" over clarity — this is a trade, not a tech company

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the design.** Every element should reinforce reliability: clear pricing, real guarantees, visible reviews, transparent processes. The site should feel like shaking hands with a competent contractor.

2. **Booking should be the path of least resistance.** CTAs should be obvious, repeated, and frictionless. Offers should lead directly to contact forms or phone calls. The yard sign program, filter saver club, and maintenance plans are conversion engines — they should be prominently featured.

3. **Local pride, not generic polish.** This is a Montana company serving Montana. Use references to the landscape, the climate, the communities. The design should feel rooted in place, not templated from a theme marketplace.

4. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every section should answer one question: "What can they do for me, and how do I get it?" No riddles, no decorative copy that restates headings, no animations that exist just to exist.

5. **Show, don't tell.** Reviews speak louder than claims. Real numbers (20+ years, 10,000+ jobs, 4.9 rating) earn trust. Financing options and maintenance programs reduce the barrier to booking. Yard signs on lawns are walking billboards — the website should celebrate that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Large tap targets for mobile users (many will be on phones in their driveway or basement). High contrast text. Clear form labels. The site must be usable by older homeowners — no tiny text, no gestures-only interactions, no content that disappears behind hovers.
